FAQ & Donation Process

Everything you need to know about blood donation

Blood Donation Process

Here's what to expect when you donate blood

1. Pre-Donation Screening

Our medical staff will conduct a brief health screening, including blood pressure checks, temperature, and hemoglobin level testing. This ensures your safety and the safety of blood recipients.

2. Registration & Health History

Complete a confidential health questionnaire about your medical history, medications, and lifestyle. This information is crucial for ensuring safe blood collection.

3. Blood Collection

A trained phlebotomist will collect about one pint (450 ml) of blood. The actual donation takes 8-10 minutes. You'll be seated in a comfortable chair.

4. Recovery & Refreshments

Rest for 10-15 minutes and enjoy refreshments. Our staff monitors your condition to ensure you feel well before you leave.

5. Follow-Up Care

Receive guidance on post-donation care, including hydration and activity recommendations. You'll also get information about your next eligible donation date.

How to Prepare for Donation

✓ Do's

  • • Eat a healthy meal before donating
  • • Drink plenty of water and fluids
  • • Get adequate sleep the night before
  • • Bring a valid ID
  • • Arrive on time for your appointment

✗ Don'ts

  • • Don't donate on an empty stomach
  • • Avoid alcoholic beverages before donating
  • • Don't donate if you feel ill or feverish
  • • Avoid strenuous exercise before donating
  • • Don't take aspirin 48 hours before donation
