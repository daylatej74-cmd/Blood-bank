The Blood Donation Process

Understanding every step from donation to transfusion

Step 1: Donor Screening & Collection

Every potential donor undergoes a comprehensive health screening including a questionnaire about medical history, travel, and lifestyle factors. A mini-physical exam checks temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels.

The actual donation takes 10-15 minutes, during which 350-450ml of blood is collected using sterile, single-use equipment. Our trained staff ensures donor comfort throughout the process.

Safety First

All equipment is sterile and used only once. You cannot contract any disease by donating blood.

Step 2: Laboratory Testing & Processing

Each donation undergoes rigorous testing for infectious diseases including HIV, hepatitis B and C, and syphilis. Advanced nucleic acid testing (NAT) can detect infections even during the window period.

Blood is also typed for ABO and Rh compatibility. Additional testing may include screening for rare antibodies and genetic markers to ensure perfect matches for patients with special needs.

Quality Assurance

99.8% of donations in high-income countries are screened following basic quality procedures, ensuring the highest safety standards.

Step 3: Storage & Distribution

Processed blood components are stored under precise conditions: red cells at 1-6°C for up to 42 days, platelets at room temperature for 5-7 days, and plasma frozen at -18°C or below for up to one year.

Our distribution network ensures blood reaches hospitals and clinics 24/7. Emergency deliveries can be made within hours, while routine transfusions are scheduled to optimize inventory management.

Critical Impact

One donation can save up to 3 lives, as blood is separated into red cells, platelets, and plasma for different patient needs.

Donation Eligibility Requirements

Ensuring the safety of both donors and recipients

Age & Weight

  • • Age: 18-65 years (16-17 with parental consent)
  • • Weight: Minimum 50kg (45kg in some countries)
  • • Good general health required
  • • No active infections or illnesses

Health Conditions

  • • Hemoglobin: ≥12.0 g/dL (females), ≥13.0 g/dL (males)
  • • No cold, flu, or infections
  • • Chronic conditions may be acceptable
  • • Pregnancy deferral: 9+ months

Lifestyle Factors

  • • Tattoo/piercing: 6-month deferral
  • • Travel to malaria areas: temporary deferral
  • • No HIV positive test history
  • • No intravenous drug use
